Ingredients

For servings 09

For the batter

  • Chickpea flour / Besan - ½ cup
  • Red chile powder - ½ tsp
  • Cumin seeds - 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder- ¼ tsp
  • Asafoetida / Hing - Pinch
  • Salt - to taste
  • Rice flour - 2 to 3 tablespoon ( optional)

For the filling

  • Medium potatoes - 2
  • Green chilies finely chopped - 2
  • Ginger grated - 1 tbsp
  • Coriander leaves / Cilantro finely chopped - 2 to 3 tbsp
  • Mustard seeds - 1 tsp
  • Urad dal - 1 tsp
  • Garlic cloves finely chopped - 3 (optional)
  • Asafoetida / Hing - small pinch
  • Turmeric powder - pinch
  • Curry leaves - 1 sprig, finely chopped
  • Salt - to taste
  • Vegetable Oil - 1 teaspoon + for frying

Preparation

    Filling

  1. Peel the skin of potatoes, cut them into cubes and wash them.
  2. Cook the potatoes in any way desired but make sure they are just firm and not mushy. I have just sprinkled water, covered with lid and microwaved
    the potatoes for 5 minutes.
  3. Alternatively, you can cook potato cubes in boiling water for 15 to 20 minutes or pressure cook it for 2 whistles or cook even in steamer or pot.
  4. In a small pan, heat oil on medium to low flame. Add mustard seeds, and when they start to splutter add urad dal, garlic cloves
    (skip if you are preparing on vrat days) and hing and sauté till the raw smell of garlic goes off (about 1 minute) and urad dal becomes light in color..
    Next add turmeric powder, curry leaves and saute for another few seconds.
  5. Mash the cooked potatoes. To this add green chilies, ginger, coriander leaves, salt and sautéed ingredients prepared above and mix well.
  6. Divide this into 9 or 10 portions or to desired size bonda or vada and make balls out of it.

    7. Batter

  7. In a bowl, add chickpea flour, red chilli powder, cumin seeds, turmeric powder, asafoetida, salt, and mix
  8. Add water little by little and whisk to form a batter that is thinner than pancake batter. See that there are no lumps and batter is not too thick or too thin.
  9. The amount of water I added was approximately ⅓ cup but not more than ½ cup. This also varies based on brand of flour, weather conditions and water.
  10. Your vadas should be coated easily in this batter. If the batter is too thick then add water, and if it becomes too watery then add chickpea flour and adjust
    accordingly.

    11. Fry vada

  11. Heat oil in a heavy bottomed pan for frying.
  12. Once the oil is hot, dip each potato ball in the batter and drop it in oil. Add 4 to 5 balls depending on the size of your pan. Deep fry them
    till they are golden brown in color
  13. Transfer this to paper towel to absorb any excess oil.
  14. Repeat the process for remaining potato balls.
  15. Batata Vadas or Aloo Bonda are now ready.
